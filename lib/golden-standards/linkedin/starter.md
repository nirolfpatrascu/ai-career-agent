## HEADLINE (220 characters max)

**Formula:** [Degree/Field] | [Specific Interest/Specialty] | [2-3 Key Skills] | [Intent/Aspiration]

### Real Examples:
- "Computer Science Graduate | Full Stack Development | React, Python, PostgreSQL | Building Products People Love"
- "Marketing Associate | Digital Marketing & Analytics | Google Analytics Certified | Driven by Data-Backed Growth"
- "Recent MBA Graduate | Product Management | Startup Mentality | Turning User Insights into Shipped Features"
- "Junior Software Engineer | TypeScript & React | Open Source Contributor | Shipped 3 Projects with 2K+ Users"
- "Business Analyst | Data Analysis | SQL, Tableau, Python | Turning Data into Decisions"

**Key rules:**
- NEVER use "Recent Graduate" or "Aspiring [Role]" as your lead — it is the weakest possible opening
- Lead with your target role or your strongest skill
- Include 2-3 technical skills that match job postings you want
- End with an outcome or differentiator, not a plea ("Seeking opportunities")
- Think: "What would make a recruiter click?" not "What describes my current situation?"

## ABOUT SECTION (2,000 characters max)

**Structure:** Hook (what you build/do) → Best achievement → Skills with proof → Learning mindset → CTA

### Golden Standard Example:

I build software that people actually use. My open-source GitHub Action for automated code review has 800+ stars and has been adopted by 50+ projects. My personal finance tracker has 500+ active users. I care about solving real problems with clean code, not just completing assignments.

This past summer at Stripe, I built an automated testing framework that reduced QA time by 35% and caught 12 bugs before they hit production. I also shipped a React dashboard used by 150+ team members within two weeks of launch. The internship taught me what production-quality engineering looks like: code review rigor, backward compatibility, and the weight of shipping to systems that process real money.

My technical toolkit includes Python, TypeScript, React, Node.js, PostgreSQL, and AWS. I have built full-stack applications from database design to deployment, and I am most energized when I can see my work being used.

Outside of code, I served as a Teaching Assistant for intro programming (30 students), organized hackathons through our CS club (200+ participants), and taught Python to high school students through Code.org. I learn best by teaching, and I thrive in teams where knowledge sharing is the norm.

Open to entry-level software engineering roles where I can contribute from day one while learning from experienced engineers. If your team values curiosity and craft, I would love to connect.

## FEATURED SECTION (4-6 items — this is YOUR portfolio)

1. **Best Project** — GitHub repo with strong README (Problem → Solution → Tech → How to run it → Results)
2. **Second Project** — Different tech stack or problem domain to show range
3. **Internship Deliverable** — If shareable: screenshot, blog post about what you built, or case study
4. **Resume** — Upload your polished 1-page resume as a PDF
5. **Blog Post** — "What I Learned During My Internship at [Company]" or "Building [Project] from Scratch"
6. **Certification** — AWS, Google, freeCodeCamp, or relevant completion badge

**Why this matters for career starters:** Your Featured section IS your portfolio. Recruiters for entry-level roles expect limited work experience but want to see evidence of initiative, learning ability, and the quality of your thinking.

## CONTENT STRATEGY

**Post frequency:** 1-2x per week (consistency matters more than volume at this stage)

**Content pillars:**
1. **Learning journey** (40%): What you're building, what you learned, mistakes you made and fixed
2. **Project showcases** (25%): Demo GIFs, architecture decisions, "how I built X" posts
3. **Industry curiosity** (20%): Thoughtful commentary on tech trends, tools you're exploring
4. **Gratitude and community** (15%): Thank mentors, share event experiences, celebrate small wins

**Format tips:**
- "I just built X. Here's what I learned" posts perform very well for early-career profiles
- Include screenshots or demo GIFs — visual content gets 3x more engagement
- Tag people who helped you (professors, internship mentors, meetup organizers) — they will engage with your post
- Engage with 10+ posts daily from people you admire — the algorithm rewards activity before it rewards content

**What to avoid:**
- "Thrilled to announce..." for minor updates
- Reposting without adding your own perspective
- Posting only when job hunting (build presence before you need it)

## RECOMMENDATIONS

**Request from (aim for 3-5):**
- Internship manager or mentor (most valuable)
- Professor or TA supervisor (academic credibility)
- Project teammate (collaboration ability)
- Hackathon or club leader (initiative and community)
- Volunteer coordinator (character and soft skills)

**Prompt them with:** "Could you mention [specific project/contribution] and what stood out about how I approached it? Any detail about my communication, problem-solving, or learning speed would be really helpful."

**Pro tip for career starters:** GIVE recommendations first. Write thoughtful recommendations for professors, classmates, and internship peers. Many will reciprocate, and the act of writing recommendations makes you more visible in their network.
