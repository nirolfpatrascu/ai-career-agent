## HEADLINE (220 characters max)

**Formula:** [Title/Growth Focus] | [Specialization] | [Key Achievement or Differentiator]

### Real Examples:
- "Driving Growth for SaaS Startups | Marketing Strategist Focused on Demand Generation & Brand Storytelling"
- "Digital Marketing Specialist | Content Strategy & SEO | HubSpot Certified | Creating Campaigns That Drive 40%+ Engagement"
- "Brand Marketing Manager | Campaign Strategy | Team Leadership | Boosted Lead Generation by 35% YoY"
- "Demand Generation Marketer | B2B SaaS | Account-Based Marketing | LinkedIn Ads Specialist | Scaling Revenue Pipelines"
- "Content Marketing Leader | SEO Strategy | Email Marketing | Data-Driven Growth | Building Brands That Convert"

**Key rules:**
- Lead with what you DO (not your title at a specific company)
- Include high-search-volume keywords: Marketing Strategy, Demand Generation, SEO, Content Marketing, Growth
- One metric or outcome phrase makes you stand out
- Avoid: "Marketing guru," "Growth hacker," "Passionate marketer" — overused and vague

## ABOUT SECTION (2,000 characters max)

**Structure:** Hook (what you drive, not who you are) → Revenue/growth proof → How you work → Team/leadership → CTA

### Golden Standard Example:

I build marketing engines that turn pipeline into revenue. Not brand awareness for the sake of impressions, but campaigns with attribution models that prove what is working and what is not.

Over the past 8 years in B2B SaaS, I have led teams that generated $4M in qualified pipeline through account-based marketing, grew organic traffic from 12K to 85K monthly sessions through content-led SEO, and managed $2.5M budgets at 3.2x blended ROAS. My proudest achievement is not any single campaign: it is building a marketing team of 8 that consistently delivers while I focus on strategy, not execution.

My approach is rooted in data and testing. I ran 200+ A/B tests in 2024, built custom attribution dashboards in Looker, and I do not recommend scaling any channel until the unit economics are proven. I have deep expertise in demand generation, content strategy, email marketing, and paid media across Google, LinkedIn, and Meta platforms. Tools in my daily stack include HubSpot, Salesforce, Google Analytics 4, Semrush, and Figma.

What I care about beyond marketing: building systems that scale, mentoring marketers who are earlier in their careers, and working with product teams to make marketing and product strategy feel like the same conversation.

If you are scaling a B2B company and need marketing leadership that thinks in revenue, not just reach, I would love to connect.

## CONTENT STRATEGY

**Post frequency:** 3-5x per week (marketers should post more — it is part of your craft)

**Content pillars:**
1. **Campaign breakdowns** (30%): What you tested, what worked, what didn't, with real numbers
2. **Marketing ops & tools** (25%): HubSpot tips, attribution challenges, automation workflows
3. **Industry POVs** (20%): Commentary on marketing trends, AI in marketing, B2B vs B2C shifts
4. **Leadership & team** (15%): Hiring, managing, mentoring marketers, building marketing culture
5. **Curated insights** (10%): Share and add commentary to others' marketing content

**Format tips:**
- Carousel posts (native documents) get highest engagement for marketing content
- "Here is what happened when we tested X" posts outperform advice posts
- Include specific numbers in the first line (algorithm and human readers both favor specificity)
- Engage heavily in comments on CMO/VP Marketing posts — this is your professional network

## RECOMMENDATIONS

**Request from:**
- VP/CMO (strategic impact and leadership)
- Sales leaders (pipeline quality and alignment)
- Direct reports (management style and mentorship)
- Agency partners (collaboration and vision)

**Prompt them with:** "Could you mention the [specific campaign/initiative] and its impact on [revenue/pipeline/growth metric]? Also appreciated if you can speak to how we worked together cross-functionally."
