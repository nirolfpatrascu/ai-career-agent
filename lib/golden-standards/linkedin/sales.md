## HEADLINE (220 characters max)

**Formula:** [Title] | [Segment: Enterprise/Mid-Market/SMB] | [Industry/Product] | [Quantified Achievement]

### Real Examples:
- "Enterprise Account Executive | B2B SaaS | Consistent 115%+ Quota Attainment | Helping Companies Scale Revenue"
- "Sales Director | B2B Sales & Account Management | Revenue Growth Strategist | Grew Territory from $0 to $1.8M ARR"
- "Account Executive | Enterprise SaaS | Complex Deal Closing | $12M Career Bookings | 2x President's Club"
- "Senior AE | Healthcare Technology | Consultative Selling | Closed Largest Deal in Company History ($2.5M TCV)"
- "VP of Sales | SaaS Revenue Strategy | Built Team from 0-12 Reps Generating $12M ARR"

**Key rules:**
- Lead with your title (recruiters search "Account Executive," "Sales Director," etc.)
- Include your segment and product type (Enterprise, B2B SaaS, Healthcare Tech)
- One power metric: quota attainment %, ARR closed, or ranking
- Do NOT write "Open to work" in the headline — use LinkedIn's dedicated feature instead
- Your headline should make prospects think "this person understands my world"

## ABOUT SECTION (2,000 characters max)

**Structure:** Value proposition (who you help + how) → Track record proof → Your methodology → What drives you → CTA

### Golden Standard Example:

I help enterprise companies solve [specific problem your product addresses] and achieve [outcome your buyers care about]. Over the past 7 years in B2B SaaS sales, I have closed $12M in total bookings with a 118% average quota attainment across 4 fiscal years.

My approach is consultative, not transactional. I won my company's largest-ever deal, a $2.5M contract with a Fortune 100 healthcare company, through a 9-month engagement involving 14 stakeholders. The key was not a perfect pitch. It was spending the first three months understanding their clinical workflow challenges before I ever presented a solution. That deal started with a question, not a demo.

I sell with structure and discipline. I use MEDDPICC to qualify, I maintain 3.5x pipeline coverage, and I reduced my average sales cycle from 90 to 62 days by partnering closely with solutions engineering and building custom proof-of-concept projects. My demo-to-close conversion rate is 41% against a team average of 28%.

What drives me is the moment a buyer realizes I am solving their problem, not selling a product. I have earned President's Club twice, but the recognition I value most is when a customer refers me to their network unprompted.

If you are in [target industry] and navigating [challenge your product solves], I would welcome a conversation. No pitch, just a genuine discussion about your goals.

## SOCIAL SELLING STRATEGY (Critical for Sales Professionals)

**Daily habits (30-45 min/day):**
1. **Engage before you pitch**: Comment on 5-10 prospect/customer posts before any outreach
2. **Value-first content**: Share insights your buyers care about, not product features
3. **Voice notes**: 20-40 second personalized voice messages on connection requests (3x higher response rate)
4. **Problem-solution-result format**: "A CTO told me her team was spending 30 hours/week on X. We helped reduce that to 5 hours. Here's how..."
5. **Never pitch in connection requests**: Connect first, nurture second, pitch never (let them come to you)

**Post frequency:** 3-5x per week

**Content pillars:**
1. **Buyer insights** (35%): Share what you're hearing from buyers, anonymized trends, market shifts
2. **Problem-solution stories** (25%): Customer success stories framed as the buyer's journey, not your sales win
3. **Industry expertise** (20%): Commentary on trends, challenges, and changes in your target vertical
4. **Sales craft** (15%): Lessons on selling, prospecting, negotiation — builds peer network
5. **Personal brand** (5%): Occasional behind-the-scenes, team wins, personal reflections

**Key metrics to track:**
- SSI (Social Selling Index) score — aim for 70+
- Profile views per week (target: 100+ when actively prospecting)
- Connection acceptance rate (target: 40%+ with personalized invites)
- Inbound messages from prospects (the ultimate measure of social selling ROI)

## RECOMMENDATIONS

**Request from:**
- Customers/Champions (most powerful — speaks to trust and impact)
- Sales managers (speaks to performance and coachability)
- Solutions engineers (speaks to collaboration on complex deals)
- Peers (speaks to teamwork and culture)

**Prompt them with:** "Could you mention the [specific deal/project] and the outcome it delivered for your team? Any detail about the sales process that stood out to you would be valuable."

**Pro tip:** Offer to write their recommendation first. Reciprocity drives faster responses and higher quality.
