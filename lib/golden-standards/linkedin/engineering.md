## HEADLINE (220 characters max)

**Formula:** [Seniority + Title] | [Core Stack/Specialization] | [Optional Achievement or Differentiator]

### Real Examples:
- "Senior Software Engineer | Full Stack (React, Node.js, AWS) | Building Scalable Systems for 2M+ Users"
- "Backend Engineer | Python, Go, Microservices | Reduced API Latency by 40% at Scale"
- "Staff Engineer | Distributed Systems & Platform Infrastructure | Ex-Stripe, Ex-Datadog"
- "Full Stack Developer | TypeScript, React, PostgreSQL | Open Source Contributor | 5K+ GitHub Stars"
- "Senior Software Engineer | 10+ Years Building Resilient Backend Systems | Java, Spring Boot, AWS, Kubernetes"

**Key rules:**
- Lead with your target role title (this is what recruiters search)
- Front-load the most in-demand keywords: React, Python, AWS, TypeScript, Go, Kubernetes
- Include one differentiator: scale metric, notable employer, or achievement
- Do NOT use "Seeking opportunities" — it signals desperation

## ABOUT SECTION (2,000 characters max)

**Structure:** Hook (first 3 lines visible) → Core narrative → Technical depth → Community → CTA

### Golden Standard Example:

I build backend systems that handle millions of requests without anyone noticing. That is the goal: infrastructure so reliable it is invisible.

Over the past 6 years, I have designed and shipped services at companies ranging from 10-person startups to Fortune 500 enterprises. My most recent work involved migrating a monolithic Rails application to a microservices architecture, reducing API response time from 450ms to 120ms while maintaining 99.97% uptime during the transition. I led the 6-person team that made it happen.

My core stack is Python, Go, and TypeScript, with deep experience in AWS (ECS, Lambda, RDS), PostgreSQL, Redis, and Kubernetes. I care about developer experience as much as user experience: I have built CI/CD pipelines that cut deployment time from 45 minutes to 8 minutes, and I treat documentation and code review as first-class engineering activities.

Beyond my day job, I contribute to open-source projects and mentor junior engineers. I have given talks at PyData NYC and local meetups on topics ranging from caching strategies to team scaling challenges.

I am always open to conversations about distributed systems, engineering leadership, or interesting technical challenges. If you are building something ambitious, let's talk.

## FEATURED SECTION (4-6 items)

1. **GitHub Profile** — Pin your best 2-3 repositories with strong READMEs (Problem → Solution → Tech Stack → Results)
2. **Technical Blog Post** — Medium/Dev.to article on a problem you solved (e.g., "How We Reduced API Latency by 70%")
3. **Conference Talk** — Recording or slides from any speaking engagement
4. **Open Source Contribution** — Link to a significant PR in a well-known project
5. **Portfolio Website** — If you have one with case studies
6. **Certification Badge** — AWS, Kubernetes, or similar

## CONTENT STRATEGY

**Post frequency:** 2-3x per week (Tuesday-Thursday optimal)

**Content pillars:**
1. **Technical deep-dives** (40%): How you solved a hard problem, architecture decisions, debugging stories
2. **Engineering culture** (25%): Code review practices, mentoring, team processes that work
3. **Industry commentary** (20%): Thoughts on new frameworks, tools, or trends (LLMs, Rust, WebAssembly)
4. **Career reflections** (15%): Lessons learned, career transitions, what you wish you knew earlier

**Format tips:**
- Native documents (PDF carousels) get highest engagement
- First line is the hook — make it stop the scroll
- End with a question to drive comments
- Engage with 10+ posts before publishing your own (algorithm rewards activity)

## RECOMMENDATIONS (Request 5-10)

**Request from:**
- Engineering managers (speaks to your impact and leadership)
- Peers (speaks to collaboration and code quality)
- Product managers (speaks to cross-functional communication)
- Mentees (speaks to your teaching ability)

**Prompt them with:** "Could you mention the [specific project] we worked on and the impact it had? Particularly the [metric/outcome]."
