## HEADLINE (220 characters max)

**Formula:** [Title] | [Specialization: LLMs/NLP/CV/RecSys] | [Key Frameworks] | [Business Impact or Scale Metric]

### Real Examples:
- "Machine Learning Engineer | Deploying Generative AI & LLMs into Production at Scale | Python, PyTorch, Kubeflow, AWS SageMaker"
- "Senior Data Scientist | Predictive Analytics & Machine Learning | Transforming Data into Market Insights | 15% Accuracy Improvement on Recommendation Engine"
- "Lead ML Engineer | Deep Learning & NLP Specialist | Building AI Solutions That Drive $8M+ Revenue Impact"
- "AI Solutions Architect | LLM Workflows, RAG, Automation | Turning AI Ideas into Shipped Products"
- "ML Engineer | Computer Vision & Object Detection | TensorFlow, PyTorch | 10M+ Daily Predictions in Production"

**Key rules:**
- Include "Machine Learning," "AI," "Data Scientist," or "ML Engineer" — these are recruiter search terms
- Name your specialization (NLP, Computer Vision, LLMs, Recommendation Systems)
- Mention 2-3 frameworks (PyTorch, TensorFlow, LangChain, Hugging Face)
- One production-scale metric differentiates you from researchers

## ABOUT SECTION (2,000 characters max)

**Structure:** Hook (what you do in production, not theory) → Track record with metrics → Technical depth → Community/research → CTA

### Golden Standard Example:

I build ML systems that work in production, not just in notebooks. Over the past 5 years, I have taken models from research prototypes to serving 10 million daily predictions with sub-50ms latency.

My most impactful work has been at the intersection of deep learning and business outcomes. I designed a dynamic pricing model that generated $8.2M in incremental revenue, built a RAG-powered AI assistant that reduced support tickets by 35%, and developed a fraud detection system that prevents $3.2M in annual losses. Each of these projects required more than model accuracy: they needed robust data pipelines, real-time serving infrastructure, monitoring for drift, and close collaboration with product and business teams.

My technical foundation is in Python, PyTorch, and TensorFlow, with production experience in MLflow, Kubeflow, SageMaker, and Kubernetes. I specialize in NLP and LLM-based applications, including fine-tuning, RAG architectures, and prompt engineering for production systems. I have also built end-to-end MLOps infrastructure, reducing model deployment cycles from weeks to hours.

I am an active contributor to the ML community. I have published 3 peer-reviewed papers (ACL, NeurIPS Workshop, EMNLP), contribute to Hugging Face Transformers, and hold Kaggle Master status with 2 gold medals. I believe the best ML engineers bridge the gap between cutting-edge research and pragmatic engineering.

Open to conversations about production ML, LLM applications, or challenging data problems. If you are building AI-native products, I would love to hear what you are working on.

## CONTENT STRATEGY

**Post frequency:** 2-3x per week

**Content pillars:**
1. **Production ML insights** (35%): Deployment challenges, monitoring, scaling lessons, inference optimization
2. **LLM/GenAI commentary** (25%): RAG architectures, fine-tuning approaches, prompt engineering, agent frameworks
3. **Research summaries** (20%): Break down new papers into practical takeaways for practitioners
4. **Career & industry** (20%): ML engineering career paths, interview prep, industry trends

**Format tips:**
- Code snippets and architecture diagrams perform well in ML content
- "What I learned deploying X" posts get high engagement
- Tag relevant researchers and practitioners to expand reach
- Share reproducible experiments with links to Colab notebooks

## RECOMMENDATIONS

**Request from:**
- ML team leads or managers (impact and leadership)
- Product managers (business translation ability)
- Research collaborators (technical rigor)
- Engineers who consumed your models (reliability and documentation quality)

**Prompt them with:** "Could you speak to the impact of [specific model/system] on [business metric]? And how [specific technical approach] made the difference?"
