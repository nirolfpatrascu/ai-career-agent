# Golden Standard Cover Letter — Career Starter (Entry-Level / Recent Graduate)

> Sources: Harvard Career Services (Mignone Center cover letter guide), Debut Careers (Graduate Cover Letter Tips & Examples), Indeed (Entry-Level Cover Letter Examples), Naukri (Cover Letter Samples 2025), Resume Worded (Entry-Level Cover Letter Examples), Zety (Entry-Level Cover Letter Guide)
> Based on patterns from university career centers and real graduates who landed first roles at competitive companies.

---

## FULL COVER LETTER

Dear [Hiring Manager Name],

I am writing to apply for the Junior Software Engineer position at [Company]. As a recent Computer Science graduate from the University of Washington, I have been following [Company]'s work on [specific product or technical challenge — e.g., "your open-source developer tools, particularly the recent improvements to your CLI framework"]. The chance to contribute to a product used by thousands of developers is exactly the kind of challenge I am looking for in my first full-time role.

During my internship at Stripe last summer, I built an automated testing framework for the payment processing module that reduced manual QA time by 35% and caught 12 regression bugs before they reached production. I also developed a real-time metrics dashboard in React and TypeScript that was adopted by 150+ operations team members within two weeks of launch. These experiences taught me how to write production-quality code under real constraints: deadlines, code review standards, backward compatibility, and the responsibility that comes with touching systems that handle real transactions.

Beyond my internship, I have built projects that demonstrate my ability to ship software that people actually use. My open-source GitHub Action for automated code review has 800+ stars and has been adopted by 50+ projects. My personal finance tracker, BudgetBuddy, has 500+ active users and was featured on Hacker News. These projects taught me full-stack development from database design to deployment, and more importantly, they taught me how to iterate based on real user feedback rather than assumptions.

I am genuinely eager to learn and grow as an engineer. I served as a Teaching Assistant for our introductory programming course, which strengthened my ability to communicate technical concepts clearly. I organized hackathons through our CS club and mentored high school students learning Python through Code.org. I bring energy, curiosity, and a habit of building things that solve real problems.

I would love to discuss how my internship experience, project portfolio, and passion for engineering could contribute to [Company]'s team. Thank you for your consideration.

Best regards,
[Full Name]

## WHY THIS COVER LETTER IS GOLDEN STANDARD

1. **Opens with genuine company knowledge**: References a specific product/initiative, not "I am excited about your company"
2. **Internship experience framed as impact, not duties**: "reduced QA time by 35%, caught 12 bugs" not "assisted the engineering team with testing"
3. **Projects compensate for limited work history**: Open-source adoption (800+ stars, 50+ projects), user traction (500+ users, Hacker News feature) — makes entry-level work feel substantial
4. **Shows learning from experience**: "taught me how to iterate based on real user feedback" — demonstrates maturity beyond years
5. **Teaching and mentoring signal communication skills**: TA role, hackathon organizing, volunteer teaching — valued soft skills for junior hires
6. **Honest about career stage**: "my first full-time role" and "eager to learn and grow" — confident about where they are without apologizing for lack of experience
7. **No generic filler**: Every sentence references a specific experience, project, or achievement
8. **Appropriate length**: 4 paragraphs, under 350 words — slightly longer than senior letters because it needs to establish credibility through multiple proof points
